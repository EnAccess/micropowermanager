# Privacy Policy

## Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how **MicroPowerManager** (“we”, “our”, “us”) collects, uses, and protects personal data through our website.
We are committed to protecting your privacy and processing your data in accordance with the **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** and the **Dutch Data Protection Act (Uitvoeringswet AVG)**.

## Information We Collect

We collect only the minimum data necessary to operate and improve our website and services. This includes:

### Information You Provide Voluntarily

When you:

- Fill out contact or inquiry forms,
- Request a demo, subscribe to updates, or communicate with us,
  we may collect your name, email address, company name, and any other details you choose to share.

### Automatically Collected Information (Performance Cookies Only)

We use **performance cookies** and similar technologies to gather **anonymous usage information** about how visitors use our website.

These cookies:

- Help us understand which pages are most visited,
- Identify and fix performance issues,
- Measure how users interact with content,
- Do **not** collect personally identifiable information.

All collected data is aggregated and used solely to improve our website’s functionality and user experience.

## How We Use Your Information

We use your information to:

- Operate and maintain our website,
- Improve website speed, usability, and performance,
- Respond to your inquiries and provide requested information,
- Analyze how our website is used (in aggregate form),
- Comply with legal obligations and ensure website security.

We **do not** use cookies or analytics to build personal profiles, deliver targeted advertising, or track users across different websites.

## Legal Basis for Processing (EEA/UK Users)

For visitors from the European Economic Area (EEA) or the UK, our legal bases for processing your data are:

- **Consent:** for the use of performance cookies (as obtained through our cookie banner), and
- **Legitimate Interest:** to maintain and improve the functionality of our website and respond to your communications.

## Sharing Your Information

We do **not** sell, rent, or trade your personal information.
We may share anonymous, aggregated analytics data with trusted service providers who help us operate and improve our website — for example, with:

- **Analytics providers** such as Google Analytics (with IP anonymization enabled) or privacy-focused tools like Plausible or Fathom.

These providers cannot identify individual visitors and are bound by strict confidentiality and data-protection agreements.

We may also disclose information:

- To comply with legal obligations or valid governmental requests, or
- To protect our rights, property, or safety.

## Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Policy or as required by law.
Anonymous analytics data may be retained for performance trend analysis but cannot be used to identify individuals.

## Your Data Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights under applicable data-protection laws:

- **Access** – request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
- **Rectification** – request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information.
- **Erasure (“Right to be Forgotten”)** – request deletion of your personal data.
- **Restriction** – request that we limit processing of your data in certain circumstances.
- **Portability** – request transfer of your data to another service provider where technically feasible.
- **Withdraw Consent** – withdraw your cookie consent at any time (via your browser settings or our cookie banner).

To exercise these rights, please contact us at <info@enaccess.org>.
We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling your request.

## International Data Transfers

Our analytics and hosting providers may process data in countries outside your own.
Where this occurs, we ensure that appropriate safeguards (such as EU Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent mechanisms) are in place to protect your data according to applicable law.

## Security

We use appropriate technical and organizational measures to safeguard your data against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction.
Since we use only performance cookies, **no sensitive or personally identifiable information** is stored in cookies or transmitted to third parties for marketing purposes.

## Third-Party Links

Our Site may contain links to external websites not operated by us. We are not responsible for the content or privacy practices of those third-party websites.
We encourage you to review the privacy and cookie policies of any external sites you visit.

## Children’s Privacy

Our Site and Service are not directed toward children under 16, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under that age.
If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

## Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect operational, legal, or regulatory changes. The updated version will be posted on this page with a revised “Last updated” date. Any significant changes will be clearly indicated on our website.
